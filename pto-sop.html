Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure outlines the guidelines for requesting and managing Paid Time Off (PTO) to ensure fair and consistent application across all departments while maintaining operational efficiency.

Scope

This policy applies to all full-time and part-time employees who have completed their probationary period.

Important Note
PTO accrual begins on your first day of employment but can only be used after completing the 90-day probationary period.

Carryover Policy
A maximum of 5 unused PTO days may be carried over to the next calendar year. Any days exceeding this limit will be forfeited on December 31st. Plan your time off accordingly!

Step-by-Step Process

Step 1: Check Your Balance
Log into the HR portal and verify your available PTO balance before submitting a request.
Step 2: Submit Request
Complete the PTO request form in the HR portal at least 2 weeks before your planned time off.
Step 3: Manager Approval
Your direct manager will review and approve/deny the request within 3 business days.
Step 4: Confirmation
You'll receive an email confirmation once your PTO is approved and reflected in the system.
Emergency PTO
For unexpected emergencies, contact your manager directly and submit the formal request within 24 hours of returning to work.

Notice Requirements

  • 1-2 days off: Minimum 1 week notice
  • 3-5 days off: Minimum 2 weeks notice
  • 1+ week off: Minimum 4 weeks notice
  • Holiday periods: Minimum 6 weeks notice

Acknowledgment Checklist

Company-Wide Blackout Dates

The following periods are designated as blackout dates when PTO requests may be limited or denied due to critical business needs:

  • Year-End Close: December 28 - January 3
  • Quarterly Reporting: Last 3 business days of each quarter
  • Annual Planning: First week of September

Department-Specific Restrictions

Some departments may have additional blackout periods based on seasonal demands. Check with your manager for department-specific guidelines.

Team Coverage Requirement
A minimum of 50% team coverage must be maintained at all times. PTO requests may be denied if approval would result in inadequate coverage.

Approval Guidelines

Managers are responsible for reviewing and responding to PTO requests in a timely and fair manner. Consider the following when reviewing requests:

  • Team coverage and workload distribution
  • Project deadlines and deliverables
  • Previously approved time off for other team members
  • Employee's remaining PTO balance
  • Business-critical periods and blackout dates

Response Timeline

Managers must respond to PTO requests within 3 business days. If a request cannot be approved, provide a clear explanation and work with the employee to find an alternative.

Fair Treatment
Apply PTO approval criteria consistently across all team members. If conflicts arise between requests, use first-come-first-served or rotating priority based on previous approvals.
